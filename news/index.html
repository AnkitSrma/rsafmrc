Regional South Asia Fire Management Resource Center (RSAFMRC)

A region of the Global Wildland Fire Network (GWFN), coordinating fire management cooperation across South Asia. A specialized Center of the family of 9 Regional Fire Management Resource Centers coordinated by the Global Fire Monitoring Center (GFMC).

Complete record

Full timeline

1989 — International Liaison Committee (ILC) formed

Following the first International Wildland Fire Conference in Boston, the ILC is formed as an advisory and liaison body to the conference series, coordinating international cooperation in wildland fire management.

1998 — SAARC Summit Declaration on Environment

The Tenth SAARC Summit in Colombo issues a declaration on environment that later informs the network's founding rationale.

2001 — Working Group on Wildland Fire established

The UN-ISDR Inter-Agency Task Force for Disaster Reduction establishes WG-4, coordinated by GFMC, and prioritizes a global network of regional wildland fire networks.

2002 — Global Wildland Fire Network created

The UNISDR Global Wildland Fire Network is created, later formally recognized as a UNISDR activity in 2004.

2003 — International Wildland Fire Summit, Sydney

The summit agrees a strategy for the future development of international cooperation in wildland fire management, consolidating regional networks.

2004 — Regional networks formally endorsed

The first meeting of the UN-ISDR Wildland Fire Advisory Group recommends official recognition of Regional Wildland Fire Networks under UNISDR, FAO, and GFMC.

2007 — Foundation meeting in Kathmandu

2–3 April: Bangladesh, Bhutan, India, Nepal, Sri Lanka, and GFMC meet in Kathmandu, Nepal, founding the network and issuing the Kathmandu Declaration.

2008 — Joint meeting of the Wildland Fire Advisory Group, Freiburg

4–7 July: the Wildland Fire Advisory Group, Global Wildland Fire Network, International Liaison Committee (ILC), and Fire Management Actions Alliance Advisory Group hold a joint meeting in Freiburg, Germany.

2009 — Joins the Pan-Asia Wildland Fire Network

1–6 February, Busan, Republic of Korea: the network becomes one of four regional networks — with Northeast Asia, Central Asia, and Southeast Asia (ASEAN) — under the Pan-Asia Wildland Fire Network.

2012 — Regional Pan-Asia/Pacific Consultation, Lalitpur

20–22 November, Lalitpur, Nepal: fire scientists, practitioners, and policy makers consult on community-based approaches to wildland fire management across the Asia-Pacific region.

2024 — NFMC hosts Regional South Asia FMRC

On 13 April 2024, the Global Fire Monitoring Center (GFMC) and the National Fire Management Center (NFMC) signed an agreement establishing the Regional South Asia Fire Management Resource Center (RSAFMRC), hosted by NFMC.

January 2026 — TRANSMIT begins

NFMC begins its collaboration with Georg-August-Universität Göttingen on TRANSMIT, a project on transboundary approaches to sustainable forest management for climate resilience and disaster risk reduction in the Himalayas, running 1 January 2026 – 31 December 2028.

2026 — ICIMOD and GFMC sign MoU on fire management in the HKH

On 7 May 2026, ICIMOD and GFMC signed a Memorandum of Understanding on collaborative fire and landscape management for environmental resilience and air quality improvement in the Hindu Kush Himalaya region, with RSAFMRC supporting capacity-building activities across the region.
