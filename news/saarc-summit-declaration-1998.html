Milestone · 1998

Tenth SAARC Summit issues Declaration on Environment

The Tenth SAARC Summit, held in Colombo in 1998, issues a Declaration on Environment — a regional political commitment that would later inform the founding rationale of the Regional South Asia Wildland Fire Network.

In 1998, the Tenth Summit of the South Asian Association for Regional Cooperation (SAARC) was held in Colombo, Sri Lanka. Among its outcomes was a Declaration on Environment, committing member states to closer regional cooperation on shared environmental challenges.

The founding of the Regional South Asia Wildland Fire Network is in line with several declarations made in international conferences and summits, among them the Declaration of the Tenth SAARC Summit, Colombo 1998, on Environment. Together with decisions taken in later meetings of the UN-ISDR Working Group on Wildland Fire (WG-4) and the UN-ISDR Wildland Fire Advisory Group / Global Wildland Fire Network, the SAARC declaration helped establish the political groundwork for regional cooperation on wildland fire management in South Asia.

At the regional level, mandated institutions and cooperation mechanisms — including regional strategies such as SAARC's — were seen as essential to incorporating Regional Wildland Fire Networks within their structures and work programmes, laying part of the foundation for the network established in Kathmandu nearly a decade later.
