Milestone · 2012

Regional Pan-Asia / Pacific Consultation on Building Advanced National and Regional Capacities in Integrated Fire Management

From 20–22 November 2012, fire scientists, practitioners, and policy makers met in Lalitpur, Nepal, to consult on community-based approaches to wildland fire management across the Asia-Pacific region.

Photo: Sundar Sharma

Faced with increasing fire occurrence and shrinking public budgets, government departments, local organizations, and forest users have had to weigh a wider range of fire management options. Community-based approaches — where civil society takes an active, proactive role in planning and carrying out fire management — were increasingly seen as more effective, less costly, and more sustainable over the long term than traditional reactive firefighting.

The Regional Pan-Asia / Pacific Consultation on Building Advanced National and Regional Capacities in Integrated Fire Management based on Participatory Involvement of Local Communities was held on this basis from 20–22 November 2012 in Lalitpur, Nepal. It brought together fire scientists, practitioners, managers, and policy makers to discuss global, regional, and national approaches to wildland fire involving local communities, and to share knowledge and good practice in community-based fire management (CBFiM) across the Asia-Pacific region and beyond.

Discussions also addressed the shifting socio-economic conditions shaping fire management in the region — from rural exodus and the abandonment of agriculture and pastoralism in some countries, to reverse ex-urban migration in others, and the changing role of traditional and modern nomadic communities. The consultation went on to develop a concept for a regional community-based fire management activity, including a draft proposal for a pilot activity in Nepal and a Regional Fire Management Center for monitoring, capacity building, and advisory services.

The consultation produced a conference booklet, a report with resolutions and recommendations, and a set of presentations from national and international participants.
