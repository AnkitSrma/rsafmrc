Complete record

Full timeline

1998 — SAARC Summit Declaration on Environment

The Tenth SAARC Summit in Colombo issues a declaration on environment that later informs the network's founding rationale.

2001 — Working Group on Wildland Fire established

The UN-ISDR Inter-Agency Task Force for Disaster Reduction establishes WG-4, coordinated by GFMC, and prioritizes a global network of regional wildland fire networks.

2002 — Global Wildland Fire Network created

The UNISDR Global Wildland Fire Network is created, later formally recognized as a UNISDR activity in 2004.

2003 — International Wildland Fire Summit, Sydney

The summit agrees a strategy for the future development of international cooperation in wildland fire management, consolidating regional networks.

2004 — Regional networks formally endorsed

The first meeting of the UN-ISDR Wildland Fire Advisory Group recommends official recognition of Regional Wildland Fire Networks under UNISDR, FAO, and GFMC.

2006 — Nepal recognized as convener

Following discussions with representatives from Nepal and India, Nepal's Department of Forests is recognized as the convening country for the South Asia network.

2007 — Foundation meeting in Kathmandu

2–3 April: Bangladesh, Bhutan, India, Nepal, Sri Lanka, and GFMC meet in Kathmandu, Nepal, founding the network and issuing the Kathmandu Declaration.

2009 — Joins the Pan-Asia Wildland Fire Network

1–6 February, Busan, Republic of Korea: the network becomes one of four regional networks — with Northeast Asia, Central Asia, and Southeast Asia (ASEAN) — under the Pan-Asia Wildland Fire Network.
